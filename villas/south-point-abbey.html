South Point Abbey

4 Bedroom Dutch Colonial Beachfront Villa — Built 1640s

Beachfront · Ahangama

South Point Abbey

South Point Abbey dates to the Dutch colonial era — built in the 1640s with walls of solid coral, and believed to have housed a native doctor to the Southern Province. The original clay tiles on the ground floor remain intact. Antique doors and windows from the Dutch and British colonial periods give the villa a character that cannot be manufactured or replicated.

The ground floor has one bedroom with ensuite bathroom, a reading room that converts to a second bedroom, a hall, and a long verandah facing the ocean. The first floor has three bedrooms with ensuite bathrooms, a comfortable living room, and a long balcony with sweeping ocean views. The villa has been lovingly restored by the owners — not renovated, but restored, with every original feature preserved.
