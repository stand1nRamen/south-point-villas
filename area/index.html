Ahangama, Sri Lanka

The South Coast

Between Galle Fort and Mirissa, Ahangama occupies the finest stretch of Sri Lanka's southern coast — ancient, beautiful, and still unhurried.

Where We Are

The Heart of the
Southern Coast

Ahangama sits at the quiet centre of Sri Lanka's most celebrated coastline. To the west, 18 kilometres away, is Galle Fort — the UNESCO World Heritage walled city built by the Dutch in the 17th century, now home to the best galleries, restaurants and shops in the south. To the east, 12 kilometres, Mirissa — the whale watching capital of the Indian Ocean and one of the most beautiful bays in Asia.

Between these two points, you have everything: world-class surf breaks, coral reefs, ancient temples, spice gardens, colonial roads lined with cinnamon and jackfruit trees, and the unhurried rhythms of a coast that has remained largely as it has been for centuries.

Ahangama itself is a working village — fishing boats at dawn, markets in the morning, the smell of fresh roti and curry in the lanes. It is not a resort strip. It is real Sri Lanka, and that is precisely why guests keep returning.

Find Us

Ahangama is on the A2 Southern Expressway coastal road, between Galle (18 km west) and Mirissa (12 km east). We are a 2-hour drive from Bandaranaike International Airport via the Southern Expressway.

18 km West

Galle Fort

Built in the 1640s by the Dutch on an earlier Portuguese fortification, Galle Fort is the best-preserved colonial sea fortress in Asia. The ramparts walk at sunset — with the Indian Ocean on three sides and the fort's whitewashed churches and merchants' houses catching the last light — is one of the great walks in the world.

Inside the fort: the Geoffrey Bawa-designed Amangalla hotel, the National Maritime Museum, Barefoot Gallery, Stick No Bills record shop, and restaurants that have drawn food writers from every continent. Half a day will never be enough. Plan a full day.

12 km East

Whale Watching at Mirissa

The waters off Mirissa are among the most reliably productive whale watching waters on the planet. Blue whales — the largest animals that have ever lived — feed in the deep water channel just offshore from November through April. Sperm whales are present year-round. Pods of spinner dolphins accompany almost every trip.

We arrange early-morning departures with reputable operators — boats that don't harass the animals and that return you to the villa for a late breakfast. This is not a theme park experience. These are wild animals in open ocean, and seeing a blue whale breach 50 metres from the boat changes your sense of the world permanently.

45 min East

Yala National Park

Sri Lanka has the highest leopard density of any country on Earth, and Yala is where you will find them. Block 1 of Yala National Park has more leopard sightings per safari than almost any other reserve in the world — these are not shy cats retreating into long grass. They sit in trees, walk the tracks, and regard the jeeps with a magnificent indifference.

Elephants, sloth bears, water buffalo, crocodiles, painted storks, and over 200 bird species share the park with the leopards. We arrange half-day and full-day safaris with experienced naturalist guides who have worked the park for decades and know where the animals are.

On the Coast

The Stilt Fishermen
& Local Life

The stilt fishermen of Sri Lanka's south coast — perched on poles driven into the reef, fishing with hand-lines at sunrise and dusk — are one of the most photographed images in the country. The stilts near Ahangama and Koggala are the most authentic remaining in the world; the fishermen still work them daily, not merely for tourists.

The coast between Ahangama and Galle is lined with cinnamon estates, coconut plantations, colonial-era churches, and lagoons where local boys swim in the late afternoon. We can arrange bicycle tours, tuk-tuk rides through the villages, and introductions to local families for the travellers who want to understand what they're looking at.

Practical Notes

Planning Your Time

Best Time to Visit

November to April is the prime season — dry, warm, calm seas, and peak whale watching. Surfers favour May to October for the larger southwest swells. The south coast runs counter-season to Colombo and the north: when it rains in Colombo, it is often sunny here.

Getting Here

Colombo Bandaranaike Airport (CMB) is 2.5 hours by the Southern Expressway. Mattala Airport (HRI) in the south is 1.5 hours — a smaller option for charter and regional connections. Our driver meets all arrivals. Galle train station is 18 km away for the scenic coastal train from Colombo.

Currency & Payments

Sri Lankan Rupees (LKR). ATMs in Ahangama and Galle accept international cards. USD and EUR are widely accepted at villas, restaurants and tour operators. Our villa rates are quoted in USD for international guests.

Combining Destinations

Many guests split their Sri Lanka trip: a week on the south coast at South Point Villas, then a few days in the hill country (Ella or Nuwara Eliya), then Kandy or the Cultural Triangle (Sigiriya, Polonnaruwa). We can advise on itineraries and arrange drivers for the full circuit.
