The Architecture

A Villa Born
from Geoffrey Bawa's Legacy

South Point Villa was designed by Murad Ismail — a protégé of Sri Lanka's most celebrated architect, Geoffrey Bawa. The result is a villa that breathes with its landscape: open-sided living areas, seamless transitions between inside and out, and a quiet elegance that only comes from someone who truly understood the tropics.

This is not a hotel. It is a home — designed with intention, furnished with care, and offered to guests who deserve the very best of what Sri Lanka has to give.

The History

Dutch Colonial Walls,
Centuries of Character

South Point Abbey dates to the Dutch colonial era — built in the 1640s with walls of solid coral, believed to have housed a native doctor to the Southern Province. The original clay tiles on the ground floor remain intact. The antique doors and windows, sourced from eras when the Dutch and later the British ruled the South, give the villa a character that cannot be manufactured.

It has been lovingly restored by the owners — not renovated, restored. Every original feature was preserved and honoured.

In & Around the Villas

Every Day, Something Remarkable

From sunrise yoga to Ayurvedic massage, Sri Lankan cooking to surfing — our staff will arrange anything the island has to offer.

Surfing & the Sea

Kathaluwa surf break is 150 metres from the beach villas. The reef in front of the beach villas is ideal for swimming and snorkelling. Popular surf spots at Ahangama, Weligama and Mirissa are within minutes.

Yoga

Morning yoga sessions with experienced instructors, set against the rhythm of the Indian Ocean. We arrange everything — you arrive and breathe.

Ayurvedic Wellness

Traditional Ayurvedic massages, herbal treatments and personalised therapies from expert Sri Lankan practitioners. Restore balance in paradise.

Cooking Demonstrations

Learn the art of authentic Sri Lankan cuisine with our in-house chefs. From spice grinding to coconut milk curries — a lesson you'll carry home.

Cultural Evenings

Arrange traditional dancers, drummers and fireworks on the beach. A Sri Lankan evening unlike anything in a hotel ballroom.

Fishing & Boat Trips

From October to April, hire traditional fishing boats from Kathaluwa for deep-sea trolling. Guided by local fishermen who know these waters.
