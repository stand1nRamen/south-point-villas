Our Story

What began as a private holiday home became seven villas and twenty years of welcoming guests as family.

The Beginning

Built for Family.
Opened to Guests.

The South Point Villas began in 2004 as private holiday homes — created by the owner as personal retreats for family and friends on Sri Lanka's beautiful southern coast. The first villa was South Point Villa, designed by renowned architect Murad Ismail, a protégé of Geoffrey Bawa, whose tropical modernism defined the finest homes in Sri Lanka for half a century.

Over the following years, the owner acquired and restored neighbouring properties — each one chosen for its character, its history, and its position on the coast. South Point Abbey, the Dutch colonial estate built with coral walls in the 1640s. The Chalet, intimate and private. South Point Ocean, the grande estate that can accommodate an entire extended family.

When guests began asking to stay, the answer was simple: come as family. That philosophy has never changed.

The Architecture

Geoffrey Bawa's Legacy
in Ahangama

Geoffrey Bawa is considered the most important architect of the 20th century to emerge from Asia. His work — from the Sri Lanka Parliament to the Heritance Kandalama hotel — defined tropical modernism and influenced architects from London to Tokyo. His belief was simple: a great building should belong to its landscape, not compete with it.

South Point Villa was designed by Murad Ismail, one of Bawa's closest protégés, and it shows: open-sided living areas that breathe with the sea breeze, seamless transitions from interior to garden to ocean, and a material palette sourced from the land itself. It is not a villa that could be placed anywhere else. It belongs here.

The Heritage

Four Centuries of
Southern Sri Lanka

South Point Abbey is one of the oldest standing structures on the southern coast. Built in the 1640s during the Dutch colonial era — walls of solid coral, clay tiles fired in the 17th century, doors and windows sourced from eras when the Dutch and later the British administered the South. The property is believed to have served as the residence of a native doctor to the province.

The owner spent years restoring the Abbey — not renovating it, restoring it. Every original feature was identified, preserved and honoured. The result is a villa where you can feel history in your hands: the thick coral walls cool even at midday, the clay tiles warm in the morning light, the antique wood of the doors polished to a quiet glow.

The Promise

One Owner.
He Answers the Phone.

Every reservation at South Point Villas is handled personally by the owner. Not a booking engine. Not a reservation team. The owner reads your enquiry, understands what you need, and replies with the right villa, the right dates, and the right plan for your stay.

When you arrive, the manager is at the gate to greet you. The chef is in the kitchen. The maids know the villa as their own home. The night watchman walks the grounds. And if you need anything at 2am, the manager's number is the first one you call — and it is answered.

This is what it means to stay at South Point Villas. Not a hotel. A home, offered to you as a guest of the family.
